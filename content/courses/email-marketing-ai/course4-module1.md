## 1.1 Email Marketing in 2025 — Still the Highest ROI Channel

Despite the rise of social media, paid ads, and messaging apps, email marketing consistently delivers the highest return on investment of any digital channel. Industry benchmarks in 2025 put average email ROI at $36–$42 for every $1 spent.

Why? Because email is:
- **Owned media:** You own your list. Algorithm changes don't affect your reach.
- **Direct:** Email lands in the inbox, not a feed that competes with hundreds of other pieces of content.
- **Personalised at scale:** Modern email platforms with AI can tailor content to individual subscribers.
- **High intent:** Subscribers chose to hear from you. This self-selection makes them more likely to convert.

**How AI has changed email marketing:**
- Copy generation: AI drafts subject lines, body copy, and CTAs in seconds
- Personalisation at scale: AI generates 1:1 personalised content for each subscriber
- Send time optimisation: AI identifies the best time to email each individual subscriber
- Predictive segmentation: AI predicts which subscribers are likely to buy, churn, or engage
- Automated journeys: AI determines which email to send next based on subscriber behaviour

## 1.2 Auditing Your Email Strategy with AI

Before building or improving an email program, understand where you stand.

**Email audit prompt:**
> I'm running email marketing for [business description]. Here's my current setup:
> - List size: [X subscribers]
> - Average open rate: [X%]
> - Average click rate: [X%]
> - Unsubscribe rate: [X%]
> - ESP (email platform): [name]
> - Email types I send: [newsletters / promotions / automated sequences / transactional]
> - Sending frequency: [X per week/month]
>
> Audit my email program. What are the biggest risks and opportunities? What benchmarks should I be hitting in 2025? What are my top 3 priorities to improve performance?

**Industry benchmarks (2025 averages):**

| Metric | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|
| Open rate | 25–35% | 40%+ |
| Click-through rate | 2–4% | 5%+ |
| Click-to-open rate | 8–12% | 15%+ |
| Unsubscribe rate | Under 0.3% | Under 0.1% |
| Conversion rate | 1–2% | 3%+ |

*Note: open rates have been inflated since Apple MPP (Mail Privacy Protection) in 2021. Click-to-open rate is now a more reliable engagement metric.*

## 1.3 List Growth Strategy

Your list is the foundation of everything. A small, highly engaged list beats a large, disengaged one.

**Building a list growth strategy with AI:**
> I need to grow my email list for [business/content type]. My target audience: [describe]. I currently get [X new subscribers per month / I'm starting from zero].
>
> Help me build a list growth strategy. Include: (1) the 3 most effective lead magnet types for my audience, (2) the best acquisition channels for my business type, (3) what I should promise subscribers in my welcome sequence, (4) how to ensure the subscribers I attract are qualified (likely to buy/engage).

**High-converting lead magnet types in 2025:**

| Type | Best For |
|---|---|
| Checklist or toolkit | Action-oriented audiences |
| Mini-course or email course | Educational content |
| Template or swipe file | Professional audiences |
| Calculator or tool | Data-driven decisions |
| Report or research | B2B audiences |
| Discount or offer | E-commerce |
| Waitlist for something desirable | Product launches |

**Lead magnet copy prompt:**
> Create a landing page for a lead magnet that offers [describe your offer]. Target audience: [describe]. The page should: have a headline that communicates the primary benefit in under 10 words, 3 bullet points of what they'll get, a simple form, and a CTA button text that's more compelling than "Subscribe" or "Sign Up."

## 1.4 Structuring Your Email Program

A well-structured email program has three components working together: broadcast emails, automated sequences, and transactional emails.

**Broadcast emails:** One-to-many sends on a regular schedule — newsletters, promotions, announcements

**Automated sequences:** Pre-written email series triggered by subscriber actions — welcome, purchase, onboarding, re-engagement, win-back

**Transactional emails:** Triggered by specific transactions — order confirmations, receipts, shipping updates, password resets

**Program structure prompt:**
> I'm building an email program for [business type]. I send [or want to send] emails for: [describe your current or planned use cases].
>
> Help me structure a complete email program. For each email type: what's the objective, what triggers it, what should it contain, and how does it fit into the subscriber journey? Prioritise by which will have the most impact on my business first.

## 1.6 Setting Goals for Your Email Program

**Goal-setting prompt:**
> I'm building / improving my email marketing program for [business]. My business goals are: [list].
> Help me set specific 90-day email marketing goals with: primary KPIs for each goal, baseline metrics I should measure now, and leading indicators I can track weekly.

**Goal → Metric mapping:**

| Business Goal | Email KPI |
|---|---|
| Increase revenue | Revenue per email sent; conversion rate |
| Improve engagement | Click-to-open rate; reply rate |
| Grow list | New subscribers per month; cost per subscriber |
| Reduce churn | Re-engagement rate; win-back conversion |
| Nurture leads | Lead-to-customer conversion rate from email sequences |

## Key Takeaways

- Email delivers **$36–42 ROI per $1 spent** — still the highest-ROI channel in 2025
- AI transforms email marketing: copy generation, personalisation, send time optimisation, predictive segmentation
- A **click-to-open rate** is now more reliable than open rate as an engagement metric
- Your list is owned media — a small, engaged list beats a large, disengaged one
- Structure your program around three pillars: **broadcast, automated sequences, and transactional emails**
- Choose your platform based on business type, integration needs, and which AI features matter most
