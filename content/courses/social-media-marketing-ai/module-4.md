## 4.1 Why Analytics Matter More Now

More content. More channels. More data. Most social media marketers are drowning in numbers that don't lead to clear action.

AI changes analytics from a reporting exercise (what happened?) into a decision-making tool (what should I do next?). The difference is asking AI not just to summarise data, but to interpret it.

---

## 4.2 The Right Metrics to Track

Vanity metrics (follower count, likes) are easy to see and rarely meaningful. Focus on metrics that connect to business outcomes.

**Metrics that matter by goal:**

**Brand Awareness**
- Reach (unique accounts)
- Impressions
- Share of voice (vs. competitors)
- Branded search volume (outside of platform)

**Engagement and Community**
- Engagement rate (interactions ÷ reach or impressions)
- Comment quality and sentiment
- Saves (highest-quality engagement signal on Instagram)
- Shares / reposts

**Traffic and Lead Generation**
- Link clicks and click-through rate
- Profile link clicks (Instagram/TikTok)
- Conversion rate from social traffic (track in Google Analytics / GA4)

**Revenue / Commercial**
- Revenue attributed to social
- Cost per lead / acquisition (paid)
- Customer lifetime value from social channels

**Setting up your tracking:**
> I'm tracking social media performance for a [business description]. My business goals are [list]. 
> Help me build a simple tracking spreadsheet with the right metrics for each platform I'm on: [list platforms].
> For each metric: what it measures, why it matters for my goals, and where to find it in the platform analytics.

## 4.3 Using AI to Analyse Your Performance Data

Most platforms export analytics as CSVs. Upload these to ChatGPT (Code Interpreter) or paste the key numbers for AI-powered analysis.

### Content Performance Analysis

**Prompt — Post-level analysis:**
> Here's my social media performance data for the last 30 days: [paste table or CSV data].
> Analyse this and tell me:
> 1. Which posts performed best by engagement rate and by reach?
> 2. What patterns do they share (topic, format, posting time, length)?
> 3. Which posts underperformed, and what might explain that?
> 4. What should I do more of and less of based on this data?

**Prompt — Format analysis:**
> Looking at this data [paste], compare the average performance of: videos vs. images vs. carousels vs. text posts. Which format consistently drives the most: reach, engagement, saves, and link clicks?

### Trend Identification

> I've been posting for 6 months. Here's my monthly performance data [paste].
> What are the trends? Is my engagement rate improving, declining, or flat? What changed in [month X] that might explain the shift? What should I focus on to improve performance over the next 3 months?

### Competitive Benchmarking

You can't see competitors' exact analytics, but you can observe their public metrics:
> Here are the social media metrics I've observed from my top 3 competitors over the last month [describe what you've seen: posting frequency, average likes/comments, content types].
> How do I compare? What are they doing well that I could learn from? What gaps do I see that I could exploit?

## 4.4 Social Listening and Brand Monitoring

Social listening means tracking what people are saying about your brand, your competitors, and your industry — beyond your own posts.

### AI-Powered Listening Tools

**Brand24** — Monitors mentions of your brand, products, or keywords across social media, news, forums, and blogs. AI generates sentiment scores and trend summaries.

**Mention** — Real-time monitoring with sentiment analysis and competitive tracking.

**Sprout Social Listening** — Enterprise-grade social listening with AI-powered trend detection and topic modelling.

**Brandwatch** — Industry-leading for large brands; AI identifies emerging topics and conversation shifts.

**Free option:** Google Alerts for brand name mentions on the web; manual monitoring on platforms.

### What to Monitor

> I want to set up social listening for my brand [name] and competitors [list]. 
> What keywords, phrases, and hashtags should I monitor? 
> Include: brand name variations, product names, competitor names, industry terms, and common customer questions or complaints related to my category.

### Turning Listening Data into Content

> Here are recent conversations about [topic/your brand] from social media [paste themes, quotes, or summaries]:
> 1. What are the most common questions people have?
> 2. What misconceptions are people spreading?
> 3. What content could I create to address these directly?
> 4. Are there any emerging trends I should comment on or create content around?

## 4.5 Reporting — Making Data Tell a Story

Analytics reports that nobody reads are worthless. The goal is insights that lead to action.

### Monthly Social Media Report Template

> Create a monthly social media performance report template for a [business type] reporting to [stakeholder type — e.g., marketing director, founder, board].
> Include sections for: executive summary, channel-by-channel performance vs. last month and vs. goals, top performing content (with examples), what we learned, and next month's strategy adjustments.
> Format: concise, visual where possible, decision-focused — not just numbers.

**After your report is drafted:**
> Here's my monthly social media data [paste]. Using the report structure above, fill in the actual figures and write the analysis sections. Highlight 2–3 clear actions we should take next month based on what the data shows.

### The "So What?" Test

Every metric in your report should pass the "so what?" test — it should connect to a decision or action.

- Reach went up 23% → so we should invest more in [content type/channel]
- Engagement rate fell → so we need to test [new format/different topics]
- Video outperforms images 3:1 → so our Q4 content calendar should be 70% video

If a metric doesn't lead to "so what we should do X," it probably doesn't belong in the report.

## 4.6 A/B Testing with AI

Systematic testing is how you improve over time. AI makes it faster and easier to design and interpret tests.

### Designing Tests

**Prompt:**
> I want to A/B test [element — e.g., captions, posting times, video length, hooks].
> Help me design a simple test: what exactly should I vary, what should I keep constant, how many posts do I need to run before the results are meaningful, and what metric should I use to determine the winner?

**Variables worth testing (one at a time):**
- Posting time (morning vs. evening)
- Hook format (question vs. statement vs. statistic)
- Post length (short vs. long)
- CTA type (question vs. directive)
- Hashtag strategy (many vs. few vs. none)
- Video length (under 30s vs. 30–60s vs. 60–90s)

### Interpreting Results

> I ran an A/B test on [what you tested]. Results: [paste performance data for each variant].
> Which variant won and by how much? Is the difference statistically meaningful given the sample size? What does this suggest I should change about my content going forward?

## Key Takeaways

- Focus on **metrics that connect to business outcomes** — reach, engagement rate, link clicks, conversions — not follower count or likes
- Upload your analytics data to ChatGPT for **AI-powered pattern analysis** — what worked, why, and what to do next
- **Social listening** tools (Brand24, Mention, Sprout) let you monitor brand, competitors, and industry conversations in real time
- Every report metric should pass the **"so what?" test** — if it doesn't lead to an action, cut it
- **A/B test one variable at a time** — hooks, posting times, formats, CTAs — and use AI to interpret results
- The best analytics aren't about the past — they're about informing your next decision
