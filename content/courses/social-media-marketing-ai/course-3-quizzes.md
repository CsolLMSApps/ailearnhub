## MODULE 4: AI FOR SOCIAL MEDIA ANALYTICS

### Question 1
**What is the correct formula for calculating engagement rate?**

A) Total Followers / Total Likes × 100  
B) (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Followers × 100  
C) Total Impressions / Total Reach × 100  
D) Clicks / Impressions × 100

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Engagement rate is calculated by adding all engagement actions (likes, comments, shares) and dividing by your total follower count, then multiplying by 100 to get a percentage. This shows what percentage of your audience actively engages with your content.

**Difficulty:** Easy

### Question 2
**What is the key difference between reach and impressions?**

A) There is no difference - they mean the same thing  
B) Reach is unique people who saw content; impressions is total times content was displayed  
C) Reach is for organic content; impressions is for paid ads  
D) Impressions is unique people; reach is total views

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Reach counts unique individual people who saw your content, while impressions count the total number of times your content was displayed (including multiple views by the same person). For example, if 100 people each see your post 3 times, you have 100 reach and 300 impressions.

**Difficulty:** Easy

### Question 3
**According to 2024 benchmarks, what is considered a "good" engagement rate on Instagram?**

A) 0.5-1%  
B) 1-3%  
C) 5-7%  
D) 10-15%

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** For Instagram in 2024, an engagement rate of 1-3% is considered good, while 3-6% is excellent. This is based on industry benchmarks and varies by niche. Smaller accounts often have higher engagement rates than larger ones.

### Question 4
**In A/B testing, what does "statistical significance" mean?**

A) The results look impressive  
B) You've tested enough data to be confident the difference isn't due to random chance  
C) Both versions performed equally well  
D) You need to run more tests

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Statistical significance means you have enough data to be confident (typically 95% confident) that the difference in performance between your two variations is real and not just due to random chance. Generally, you need at least 100 engagements per variation and 7+ days of testing.

**Difficulty:** Medium

---

### Question 5
**What is the basic ROI formula for social media marketing?**

A) Revenue - Cost  
B) (Revenue - Cost) / Cost × 100  
C) Revenue / Cost  
D) Cost / Revenue × 100

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** ROI (Return on Investment) is calculated as: (Revenue - Cost) / Cost × 100. This gives you a percentage that shows how much return you're getting for every dollar spent. For example, if you spend $100 and make $300, your ROI is 200%.

**Difficulty:** Easy

---

### Question 6
**When running an A/B test, which best practice should you follow?**

A) Change multiple variables at once to save time  
B) Change only ONE variable at a time  
C) Run tests for just 24 hours  
D) Only test with small audiences

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** The fundamental rule of A/B testing is to change only one variable at a time. If you change multiple things (caption AND image AND posting time), you won't know which change caused the difference in performance. This is called isolating variables.

**Difficulty:** Easy

### Question 7
**What does CPA stand for in social media marketing, and what does it measure?**

A) Cost Per Action - measures any user action  
B) Cost Per Ad - measures advertising costs  
C) Cost Per Acquisition - measures cost to acquire one customer  
D) Cost Per Analysis - measures analytics costs

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:** CPA stands for Cost Per Acquisition and measures how much you spend to acquire one customer through social media. It's calculated by dividing total social media costs by the number of customers acquired. This is a critical metric for determining marketing profitability.

**Difficulty:** Medium

---

### Question 8
**Which attribution model gives equal credit to all touchpoints in the customer journey?**

A) Last-click attribution  
B) First-click attribution  
C) Linear attribution  
D) Time-decay attribution

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:** Linear attribution distributes credit equally across all touchpoints in the customer journey. If a customer saw an Instagram ad, visited your website, saw a Facebook retargeting ad, then purchased, each touchpoint gets 33.3% credit. This is useful when all touchpoints are equally important.

**Difficulty:** Medium

---

### Question 9
**What is the minimum recommended duration for running an A/B test to achieve reliable results?**

A) 24 hours  
B) 3 days  
C) 7 days  
D) 30 days

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:** A/B tests should run for at least 7 days to account for day-of-week variations in user behavior and to gather sufficient data. Some days (like weekends) may perform differently than others, so testing across a full week gives more reliable results.

**Difficulty:** Easy

---

### Question 10
**Which metric is most important when your primary goal is brand awareness (not sales)?**

A) Cost per acquisition  
B) Conversion rate  
C) Reach and impressions  
D) Click-through rate

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:** For brand awareness goals, reach (unique people who saw your content) and impressions (total views) are the most important metrics. These show how many people you're exposing to your brand. Conversion metrics like CPA are more important for direct response campaigns.

**Difficulty:** Medium

## MODULE 5: AI-POWERED COMMUNITY MANAGEMENT

### Question 11
**Which type of comment should NEVER be automated and always receive a personal response?**

A) "Great post!"  
B) Customer complaints or negative feedback  
C) "Love this!"  
D) Emoji-only comments like "❤️😍"

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Customer complaints or negative feedback should always receive personal, empathetic responses. Automated responses to complaints can make situations worse and damage your brand reputation. These require human judgment, empathy, and the ability to resolve unique situations.

**Difficulty:** Easy

### Question 12
**What is the "80/20 rule" in comment management?**

A) Respond to 80% of comments, ignore 20%  
B) 80% of comments fall into 10 predictable categories that can be automated  
C) 80% should be automated, 20% personal  
D) Spend 80% of time on comments, 20% on content creation

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** The 80/20 rule in comment management means that approximately 80% of comments fall into about 10 predictable, repetitive categories (thank yous, common questions, etc.) that can be safely automated, while the meaningful 20% require personal attention.

**Difficulty:** Medium

### Question 13
**In sentiment analysis, what are the three primary sentiment categories?**

A) Happy, sad, angry  
B) Positive, neutral, negative  
C) Good, bad, neutral  
D) Love, hate, indifferent

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Sentiment analysis categorizes comments and mentions into three primary categories: Positive (compliments, praise, satisfaction), Neutral (questions, information requests), and Negative (complaints, concerns, criticism). This helps prioritize responses and track overall brand sentiment.

**Difficulty:** Easy

### Question 14
**What should you do FIRST when you detect a potential PR crisis on social media?**

A) Delete all negative comments  
B) Post a defensive response immediately  
C) Acknowledge the issue and say you're investigating  
D) Ignore it and hope it goes away

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:** The first step in crisis management is to quickly acknowledge the issue (within 1 hour) and let people know you're investigating. This shows you're aware and taking it seriously. Never delete comments or go silent, as this typically makes crises worse.

B) Casual and humorous

A) It's required by law  
B) Influencers receive dozens of pitches daily and ignore obvious mass emails

### Question 23
**Which ad copy framework follows the structure: Problem → Agitate → Solution?**

A) AIDA  
B) PAS  
C) BAB  
D) 4 Ps

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** PAS stands for Problem, Agitate, Solution. This framework identifies a problem your audience has, agitates it (makes them feel the pain more intensely), then presents your product/service as the solution. It's highly effective for pain-point-focused marketing.

**Difficulty:** Medium

### Question 24
**What is Dynamic Creative Optimization (DCO)?**

A) Using one perfect ad creative  
B) Automatically testing combinations of multiple ad elements (headlines, images, CTAs)  
C) Manually creating 100 ad variations  
D) Copying competitor ads

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** DCO automatically tests different combinations of ad elements (headlines, images, text, CTAs) to find the best-performing combinations. For example, testing 5 headlines × 5 images × 3 CTAs = 75 combinations, with the platform optimizing delivery to top performers.

**Difficulty:** Medium

### Question 25
**When adapting content for different platforms, what's most important?**

A) Post identical content everywhere to save time  
B) Make each piece feel native to the platform while maintaining core message  
C) Only post on one platform  
D) Use different branding on each platform

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Each platform has unique user behavior, content formats, and expectations. While your core message should be consistent, the format, tone, and presentation should feel native to each platform. TikTok content should feel different from LinkedIn, even if promoting the same thing.

### Question 26
**In multi-touch attribution, what does "time-decay" attribution mean?**

A) All touchpoints get equal credit  
B) Only the first touchpoint gets credit  
C) Recent interactions get more credit than earlier ones  
D) Only the last touchpoint gets credit

**Correct Answer:** C

**Explanation:** Time-decay attribution gives more credit to touchpoints closer to the conversion. The logic is that recent interactions (like a retargeting ad right before purchase) had more influence than earlier awareness touchpoints (like seeing your brand on Instagram weeks ago).

**Difficulty:** Medium

### Question 30
**What is Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) and why does it matter for social media marketing?**

A) The cost to acquire a customer; it determines ad budgets  
B) The total revenue a customer generates over their entire relationship with your business; it determines how much you can spend acquiring them  
C) The number of times a customer visits your website  
D) The engagement rate of loyal customers

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) is the total revenue you'll earn from a customer over their entire relationship with your business. If your average LTV is $500, you can afford to spend more on acquisition (higher CPA) than if your LTV is $50. This is crucial for determining sustainable marketing budgets.

**Difficulty:** Medium
