## 6.1 Trend Spotting and Newsjacking with AI

Trends are time-sensitive opportunities. The brands that respond fastest to emerging conversations get the most organic reach.

### Monitoring Trends with AI

**Tools for trend detection:**
- **Google Trends** — free; shows search interest over time; set alerts for your keywords
- **Exploding Topics** — identifies trends before they peak; excellent for content planning
- **TikTok Creative Centre** — shows trending sounds, hashtags, and content in your niche
- **X (Twitter) Trending** — real-time cultural moments and news
- **Perplexity** — ask "what's trending in [your industry] this week?" for sourced current answers

**Daily trend monitoring prompt:**
> What are the most significant trends, news stories, or cultural moments happening this week in [your industry/niche]? For each, tell me: (1) what it is, (2) why it's resonating, (3) whether it's appropriate for [brand type] to engage with, and (4) a specific content idea if we should.

### Newsjacking — Joining the Conversation

Newsjacking means inserting your brand into a trending conversation relevantly and quickly.

**Before newsjacking, use AI to sense-check:**
> A trending topic in our space is [topic]. 
> My brand is [describe]. 
> Should I create content around this trend? Assess: (1) is this relevant to my audience, (2) are there any risks or sensitivities I should be aware of, (3) what angle would feel authentic rather than opportunistic, (4) what format would work best and how quickly should I post?

**If you proceed:**
> Create a [format] for [platform] about [trend/topic] from the perspective of [brand/your angle]. 
> It should feel: timely but not forced, relevant to our audience [describe], and contribute something new to the conversation rather than just jumping on it.

## 6.2 Building an Automated Content Pipeline

An automated pipeline means content keeps flowing even when you're not actively creating. AI is the engine.

### The Editorial Calendar System

**Monthly planning session (use at the start of each month):**
> It's the beginning of [month]. I create content for [platform(s)] [frequency per week].
> My content pillars: [list].
> Upcoming dates/events relevant to my audience: [list].
> Create a full month's content calendar with: date, platform, pillar, topic, format, and a one-line brief. Ensure variety in format and pillar. Flag any posts that are time-sensitive.

### Batching Content Production

Instead of creating daily, batch weekly or monthly:

**One 3-hour session per week can produce:**
- 10 social media posts (copy + image brief)
- 2 short-form video scripts
- 4 story/reel ideas
- 1 newsletter section

**Batching prompt:**
> I'm batch-producing content for [week/month]. Topic theme: [X].
> Generate [X] [platform] posts, each with a different angle on this theme. Vary the formats: include [X] with storytelling, [X] with tips, [X] with data or statistics, [X] with opinion.
> Use these examples of my voice as a guide: [paste 2–3 past posts].

### Automation Tools for Scheduling

Once content is created, automate the publishing:
- **Buffer** — clean, simple; posts to all platforms; AI caption suggestions; from $6/month
- **Later** — Instagram/TikTok specialist; visual calendar; link-in-bio; from $18/month
- **Hootsuite** — enterprise-grade; best for teams; AI content suggestions; from $99/month
- **Metricool** — affordable all-in-one; includes analytics; from $22/month

**Set up a repeating content queue:** Tools like Buffer's "Queue" feature let you load evergreen posts that cycle automatically — content keeps posting even when you're not actively scheduling.

## 6.3 AI-Enhanced Paid Social Strategy

Beyond the AI ad systems covered in Module 3, there are advanced tactics for maximising paid social ROI.

### Creative Testing at Scale

**The volume principle:** Meta's AI needs 3–5 creative variants minimum to optimise. High-performing accounts test 10–20 creatives per campaign to find winners quickly.

**Generating creative variants with AI:**
> I'm running ads for [product/service] targeting [audience]. 
> Current top-performing creative concept: [describe].
> Generate 8 variations of this creative using: (2) different hooks, (2) different CTAs, (2) different formats (image vs. video concept), (2) different angles (benefit-led vs. problem-led). For each, give me: headline, primary copy, and a visual brief.

### Lookalike and Interest Targeting (Where Still Relevant)

While AI targeting is taking over, lookalike audiences remain useful for accounts with large customer lists.

> I want to build the most effective custom audience for Meta ads. My customer list has [X] customers. 
> Advise me on: the optimal size for a seed audience for lookalike creation, which customer segment to use as a seed audience (e.g., top 25% by revenue), and how to layer additional constraints to ensure quality.

### Retargeting Sequences

> I want to build a retargeting sequence for people who visited [specific page] on our website but didn't convert.
> Design a 3-stage retargeting sequence: (1) awareness/reminder (day 1–3), (2) consideration/value (day 4–10), (3) urgency/offer (day 11–14).
> For each stage: ad objective, copy angle, format recommendation, and call to action.

## 6.4 Integrating Social AI with Your Marketing Stack

Social media doesn't exist in isolation. The most effective AI marketing systems connect social data to your broader tools.

### CRM Integration

Connect social leads and engagement data to your CRM:
- **HubSpot** — native social monitoring and lead capture; connects social interactions to contact records
- **Salesforce Social Studio** — enterprise social integration with Sales Cloud
- **Zapier** — connect any social tool to your CRM automatically (new Instagram lead → Salesforce contact)

### Email and Social Synergy

> I'm running an email campaign about [topic] to my list this week. 
> How should I coordinate my social media content to amplify this? 
> Give me: a social posting schedule that supports the email sends, content that extends the conversation without duplicating it, and ways to use social to re-engage subscribers who didn't open.

### Social Proof → Website

User-generated content and positive social activity can be repurposed on your website:
- Embed positive tweets and posts on landing pages
- Use customer TikToks and Instagram Reels as product page video
- Surface Instagram hashtag galleries with tools like TINT or Taggbox

## 6.5 Measuring AI Marketing ROI

The ultimate measure of your AI-powered social media investment is business impact.

### Attribution — Connecting Social to Revenue

> I want to understand the ROI of my social media marketing. My business generates revenue through [ecommerce/leads/direct contact].
> Help me set up a simple attribution system: what tracking do I need, where do I add UTM parameters, and how do I report on revenue from social in GA4?

**Basic UTM structure for social posts:**
- utm_source: platform name (instagram, linkedin, tiktok)
- utm_medium: organic or paid
- utm_campaign: campaign name or content pillar
- utm_content: post format or creative variant

### The Time-Value Calculation

> Before AI, my social media content production took [X hours per week].
> Now, with AI, it takes [Y hours per week].
> I earn [or my team costs] approximately [$/hour].
> Calculate the annual time value of the AI efficiency gain. Then help me calculate the quality-adjusted value if AI also improved my reach by [X%] and engagement by [Y%].
