## 4.1 AI for Professional Writing

Writing is where most knowledge workers spend a disproportionate amount of time. AI can cut that time by 50–70% while improving quality.

### The Writing Workflow with AI

**Don't ask AI to write for you — ask it to help you think, then draft:**

1. **Brain dump your ideas** (2 minutes, no structure)
2. **Ask AI to organise:** *"Here are my rough ideas: [paste]. Organise these into a logical structure for a [blog post / report / proposal]."*
3. **Ask AI to draft** the section you're struggling with most
4. **Edit in your own voice** — this is where your value as a human writer adds up
5. **Ask AI to proofread:** *"Check this for clarity, grammar, and any awkward phrasing."*

### Types of Work Writing AI Handles Best

**Reports and proposals:**
> Write an executive summary for a report recommending that our company adopt a hybrid work policy. Key findings: [list]. Audience: senior leadership. Tone: confident, data-driven, concise. Max 200 words.

**Professional emails:**
> Draft a follow-up email to a sales prospect I met at a conference last week. We discussed their challenges with [topic]. I want to suggest a 30-minute call and reference the solution we discussed. Warm but professional tone.

**Performance reviews:**
> Help me write a self-assessment for my annual review. My role is [role]. Key achievements this year: [list]. Areas I'm developing: [list]. Our company values are: [list]. Tone: confident, specific, concise.

**Meeting agendas:**
> Create an agenda for a 60-minute team meeting to discuss Q3 results and plan Q4 priorities. We have 8 attendees. Include time allocations and what we need to decide vs. just discuss.

## 4.2 AI for Research and Analysis

### Deep Research in Minutes

What used to take half a day of Google searching can now take 15 minutes with AI.

**Competitive analysis:**
> I'm preparing a competitive analysis of the UK project management software market. Key players: [list]. Dimensions to cover: pricing models, target customer size, key features, and weaknesses. Present as a structured comparison.

**Summarising documents:**
Upload a PDF report, research paper, or lengthy document and ask:
> Summarise the key findings of this report in 5 bullet points. What are the 2–3 most important implications for a small business in the retail sector?

**Understanding data:**
Paste a table of numbers or upload a spreadsheet and ask:
> Analyse this sales data. What are the key trends? Which products are underperforming? What do you recommend we focus on next quarter?

### Tools for Research in 2025

- **Perplexity AI** — AI-powered search with citations; ideal for research that needs current sources
- **ChatGPT with Browse** — web search + AI synthesis; great for quick market research
- **Gemini Deep Research** — autonomously researches topics over 30+ minutes; produces detailed reports with sources
- **NotebookLM (Google)** — upload your own documents and have AI answer questions about them; excellent for internal research

## 4.3 AI for Meetings

Meetings are expensive. AI is making them more efficient at every stage.

### Before the Meeting

> I have a 45-minute budget review meeting with the CFO and two department heads. My objective is to secure approval for a $50K software investment. What are the most persuasive ways to present the ROI case? What objections should I prepare for?

### During the Meeting

**AI transcription and notes** — Tools like Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai, and Microsoft Copilot in Teams automatically transcribe your meetings in real time. By the end of the call, you have a full transcript, summary, and action item list — without anyone taking notes.

These tools work with Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, and phone calls.

### After the Meeting

Paste a meeting transcript into ChatGPT or Claude and ask:
> Summarise this meeting: key decisions made, action items with owners, unresolved questions, and next steps. Format as a clear meeting recap ready to send to all attendees.

## 4.4 AI for Presentations

### Building Slides Faster

**Generate a structure first:**
> I need to present our Q2 marketing results to the board. The audience is non-technical. Key results: [list]. Create a slide-by-slide outline for a 15-minute presentation. Include what visuals or data would work on each slide.

**Write slide content:**
> Write the speaker notes for this slide: [slide title and bullet points]. The audience is senior executives. Keep each note to 3–4 sentences — enough to guide my speaking without reading word for word.

### AI Presentation Tools in 2025

- **Gamma.app** — describe your presentation in plain text; AI builds a fully designed deck in seconds
- **Beautiful.ai** — smart slide design that adjusts layout automatically
- **Microsoft Copilot in PowerPoint** — generate, edit, and redesign presentations with AI built in
- **Google Slides + Gemini** — AI writing assistance and design suggestions within Slides

## 4.5 AI for Project Management

### Project Planning

> I'm launching a new company website. Timeline: 16 weeks. Team: 1 project manager, 2 developers, 1 designer, 1 content writer. Create a project plan with phases, key milestones, dependencies, and a risk log with mitigation strategies.

### Status Reports

> Write a project status report for stakeholders. Status: on track. Key updates this week: [list]. Upcoming milestones: [list]. Risks to flag: [one]. Keep it to one page, professional tone.

### Problem Solving

> My team has missed two consecutive sprint goals. Contributing factors: unclear requirements, an underperforming team member, and unexpected technical debt. What are the most effective approaches to get back on track?

## 4.6 Industry-Specific AI Use Cases

AI has specific high-value applications in almost every industry:

**Marketing:** Campaign briefs, audience personas, copy variations, social media calendars, performance analysis

**Finance:** Data interpretation, report narrative, budget commentary, financial model documentation

## 4.8 Navigating AI at Work — Policies and Ethics

Before using AI at work, check:

1. **Does your company have an AI policy?** Many organisations now have guidelines on which tools are approved and what data can be shared.
2. **Client confidentiality:** Never paste client names, personal data, or confidential project details into consumer AI tools.
3. **Disclosure:** Some roles, industries, and clients require disclosure when AI is used in deliverables. Know your obligations.
4. **Quality control:** AI output must be reviewed before it leaves your desk. You are responsible for what you submit.
