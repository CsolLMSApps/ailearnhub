## 6.1 Your Personal AI Toolkit

There's no single "best" set of AI tools — the right toolkit depends on what you do. Here's a framework for building yours:

### The Core Stack (Everyone Needs This)

**1. A general AI assistant:** ChatGPT (most popular), Claude (best for long documents), or Gemini (best for Google integration). Pick one and use it daily for at least 4 weeks before adding others.

**2. An AI search tool:** Perplexity AI (free) for research that needs current, cited sources. Add to your browser as a default or shortcut.

**3. An AI image tool:** DALL-E 3 (built into ChatGPT Plus) or Adobe Firefly (free tier) for creating visuals.

### Add Based on Your Role

**Writers and content creators:** Grammarly (AI-enhanced editing), Hemingway Editor, Claude for long-form work

**Marketing and social media:** Buffer or Hootsuite with AI scheduling, Canva AI (Magic Design, Text to Image), ChatGPT for copy

**Business professionals:** Microsoft 365 Copilot (if your company uses Office), Otter.ai for meeting notes, Notion AI for knowledge management

**Developers and technical users:** GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Claude for code review

**Designers and creatives:** Adobe Firefly, Midjourney, Runway, Canva AI

**Students and researchers:** NotebookLM (upload papers, ask questions), Perplexity for research, Claude for essay feedback

## 6.4 Building a Prompt Library

Your prompt library is one of the most valuable things you can build as an AI user. It's a collection of prompts that work reliably for tasks you do regularly.

### How to Build It

Start simple — a note in Notion, Apple Notes, or even a Google Doc with categories:

**Work Writing**
- Meeting recap: *"Summarise this meeting transcript. Key decisions: []. Action items with owners: []. Next steps: []."*
- Status update: *"Write a brief project status update. Status: []. Key updates: []. Risks: []. Next milestones: []."*

**Research**
- Quick summary: *"Summarise [topic] in 5 bullet points for someone who knows nothing about it."*
- Comparison: *"Compare [A] vs [B] across these dimensions: []. Present as a table."*

**Personal**
- Meal planner: *"I have [ingredients]. Suggest 3 dinners for 2 people under 30 minutes."*
- Decision helper: *"Help me think through this decision: [context]. My priorities are: [list]."*

Add a new prompt whenever you write one that works really well. You'll have a library of 50+ within a month.

## 6.5 Common Beginner Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

**Mistake 1: Accepting the first response**
The first answer is a starting point. Always ask for a revision, an alternative, or an expansion. The best outputs usually come from the 3rd or 4th turn.

**Mistake 2: Vague prompts**
"Write something about marketing" → bad. "Write a 3-paragraph LinkedIn post for a small business owner announcing a new service launch, targeting local clients, casual but professional tone" → good.

**Mistake 3: Treating AI as a search engine**
AI doesn't browse the internet by default (unless you use Perplexity or ChatGPT's browse feature). Don't ask for today's news, current prices, or live data without using a tool that has web access.

**Mistake 4: Sharing confidential data**
Never paste client names, company financials, personal health data, or passwords into a consumer AI tool. Use enterprise-grade tools with data protections for sensitive information.

**Mistake 5: Not iterating**
Most people get one okay response and stop. Power users treat AI like a back-and-forth conversation. Keep going until the output is actually what you need.

**Mistake 6: Using only one tool**
Different AI tools have different strengths. ChatGPT for speed and versatility. Claude for long, nuanced work. Gemini for real-time search. Perplexity for research. Know when to switch.
