## Part 1: Email Mastery (30 Templates)

### The Email Writing Framework

Every business email needs:
1. **Clear subject line** (tells recipient what to expect)
2. **Professional greeting** (sets the tone)
3. **Purposeful opening** (why you're writing)
4. **Concise body** (the actual message)
5. **Clear action item** (what happens next)
6. **Professional closing** (maintains relationship)

**ChatGPT can nail all 6 every time.**

### Category 1: Cold Outreach Emails

**Template 1: Initial Cold Email**
```
Write a cold outreach email for [describe what you're offering].

CONTEXT:
- Recipient: [title/industry]
- Pain point they have: [specific problem]
- How we solve it: [your solution]
- Credibility: [why they should listen]

FORMAT:
- Subject line (8 words max, creates curiosity)
- 100-150 words total
- 3 short paragraphs
- Clear call-to-action

TONE: Professional, value-focused, not salesy
```

**Use when:** Reaching out to potential clients, partners, or collaborators you don't know yet.

---

**Template 2: Follow-Up After No Response**
```
Write a follow-up email to someone who hasn't responded to my initial 
outreach from [timeframe] ago.

CONTEXT: 
- Original email was about: [topic]
- Value proposition: [reminder]
- New information to add: [if any]

FORMAT:
- Subject line references original email
- Acknowledge they're busy
- Briefly re-state value
- Provide easy out ("If timing isn't right...")
- 75-100 words

TONE: Persistent but respectful, understanding
```

**Use when:** It's been 5-7 days since first email with no response.

## Pro Tips for Business Email

### Tip #1: Subject Line Mastery
✅ Good: "Q4 Budget Proposal - Review Needed by Friday"
❌ Bad: "Budget"

**Formula:** [Topic] - [Action Needed] - [Deadline/Urgency]

---

### Tip #2: The 3-Sentence Rule
If you can't explain it in 3 sentences, you need a meeting, not an email.

---

### Tip #3: Call-to-Action Clarity
Every email should answer: "What happens next?"
- "Please review by Friday and reply with approval"
- "Let me know 3 time slots that work for you"
- "No response needed, FYI only"

---

### Tip #4: The 24-Hour Rule for Emotional Emails
If you're upset:
1. Have ChatGPT draft it
2. Save as draft
3. Review in 24 hours
4. Edit tone if needed

ChatGPT keeps you professional when emotions run high.

---

### Tip #5: Template Library
Save your best emails as templates in ChatGPT:
```
"Use this email as a template style for future [type] emails: [paste example]"
```

Then reference it: "Write a similar email for [new situation]"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake #1: Too Formal or Too Casual
Match the recipient's style. If they write casually, don't be overly formal.

**Fix:** Tell ChatGPT: "Match this person's communication style: [paste their email]"

---

### ❌ Mistake #2: Burying the Lead
Get to the point in the first 2 sentences.

**Fix:** "Lead with the ask/main point, then provide context"

---

### ❌ Mistake #3: No Clear Action Item
Recipient doesn't know what to do next.

**Fix:** Always end with "Next steps: [specific actions]"

---

### ❌ Mistake #4: Writing in Paragraphs
Dense paragraphs don't get read.

**Fix:** "Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max) and bullet points"

---

### ❌ Mistake #5: Passive Voice
"A decision was made" vs. "We decided"

**Fix:** "Use active voice throughout"

## Glossary

**Cold Outreach:** Initial contact with someone you don't have a prior relationship with, typically for business development or networking.

**Call-to-Action (CTA):** Clear instruction about what the recipient should do next (reply, approve, attend, review, etc.).

**Executive Summary:** Concise overview of a longer document designed for senior leaders to quickly understand key points and make decisions.

**Active Voice:** Writing style where the subject performs the action ("We decided" vs. "A decision was made"). More direct and clear.

**Escalation:** Bringing an issue to higher authority when standard resolution attempts haven't worked.

**Delegation:** Assigning tasks or responsibilities to team members with clear expectations and support.
